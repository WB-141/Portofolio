Hobbies

Cat Parent

Being a cat parent means embracing quiet companionship, daily curiosity, and those unexpected moments of affection that only a feline can give. It teaches patience, builds routines, and fills your life with calm joy, from gentle purring to spontaneous zoomies. There's something special about earning a cat’s trust because it’s not given easily and that makes it even more meaningful.

Cooking

I like to think of myself as a pretty good cook. I don’t always follow recipes to the letter. Sometimes I improvise, sometimes it works, and sometimes it’s a learning experience. Cooking for me is part creativity, part chaos, and part cleaning up a bigger mess than I intended. But there’s something really satisfying about bringing ingredients together and making something people actually enjoy, even if I still occasionally burn the toast.

Crossfit

I like to think I’m great at CrossFit. Honestly, I doubt it most days. I show up, I sweat, and I do my best to keep up without tripping over a kettlebell. Some workouts feel like I'm thriving, others feel like survival. But there’s something addictive about pushing your limits alongside a bunch of other exhausted people who also pretend they know what they’re doing. And even if I’m not the strongest in the room, I’m definitely the most committed to pretending I enjoy burpees.

Digital Creativity

For me, coding is just another form of creativity, where logic meets imagination. I started by experimenting with Python and C#, building small games and automating random tasks just for the fun of it. Every line of code felt like crafting a little puzzle. Now with web development, I get to bring those ideas to life visually. Whether I’m designing clean interfaces or making silly scripts, I’m most at home when I’m building something from scratch that didn’t exist a few hours ago, and learning something new in the process.
